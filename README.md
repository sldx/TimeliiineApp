***Time isn't a grid***

A horizontal, infinite-canvas calendar for Mac. Pan, zoom, drag — like Figma, for time.

<BR>
 
<BR>
 

### See the shape of your time

Most calendars chop your year into 7-column boxes. TimeLiiine puts it on one continuous canvas. Zoom out, a year fits on screen. Zoom in, a single day opens up into hourly Gantt lanes. Same canvas, different scale.

You stop asking *what's on Tuesday* and start asking *where am I in the year*.

### Drag. Drop. Done.

Click and drag on the canvas → event. Resize with your cursor. Move across days. The way it should've always worked.

### Lanes, not boxes

Overlapping events sit on parallel rows. You can finally *see* the conflict instead of guessing at it.

### One click, any alert

Tap "1 hour before." Tap "1 day before." Tap "1 month before." Stack as many as you want. No nested menus. No "custom alert" modal.

### Always on. Never in the way

Lives semi-transparent on your desktop. Hover or focus → fully opaque. There when you need it, invisible when you don't.

### Bring your own calendars

Google. iCloud. CalDAV. TimeLiiine is the front end you wish they had.


### Made for

Trips, getaways, anything multi-day where you want to *feel* the shape of it. Side-by-side projects with overlapping deadlines. Brains that think in shapes and distances rather than rows and columns.
